Key Crypto Concepts & The End-to-End Flow

Let's establish a common understanding of the foundational terms and how value moves in the stablecoin ecosystem.

Fiat: Your Everyday Currency

Think of Fiat as any government-issued currency, like the US dollar, Euro, or Japanese Yen. It's centralized and controlled by a central bank.

Stablecoin: A Digital Dollar on the Internet

A Stablecoin is a type of cryptocurrency whose value is "pegged" to another asset, usually a fiat currency. For example, USDC is designed to always be worth one US dollar, backed by reserves. It's not for speculation, but for fast, stable transactions.

Blockchain: A Shared, Transparent Ledger

The Blockchain is a distributed digital ledger where all stablecoin transactions are recorded. Imagine it as a public, unchangeable "notebook" where every transaction is logged and visible to everyone, creating trust without a central intermediary.

Crypto Wallet: Your Digital Safe

A Crypto Wallet is a digital tool for storing cryptocurrencies. A custodial wallet is managed by a third party (like Bridge), holding the private keys for you. A non-custodial wallet gives you full control over your private keys and funds, but also full responsibility for security.

Bridge's Foundational Product Capabilities

These are the core APIs that underpin all of Bridge's solutions, simplifying complex money movement workflows.

Transfers API

For one-time transactions: on-ramps (fiat to crypto), off-ramps (crypto to fiat), or crypto-to-crypto conversions across various rails.

Liquidation Addresses

A permanent, automated route for recurring transactions, linking a blockchain address directly to an external bank account or another crypto address.

Virtual Accounts

Sub-accounts of Bridge's main bank account, uniquely provisioned for end customers to easily identify incoming fiat deposits for on-ramping.

Wallets API

Enables developers to create and manage secure custodial wallets for their users or company treasury, handling technical and security complexities.

Qualification Questions: Identifying the Right Fit

Ask these questions:
  • How are you currently moving money between countries?
  • How frequently are you transferring money?
  • Are your customers able to get access to dollar-based bank accounts?
  • Do you issue cards? Do your customers need ways to spend funds in local currency? Where?
Listen for this pain:
  • Multi-vendor setups, many intermediaries, long settlement times, high FX cost, currency fluctuation.
  • Inability to get access to dollars, complicated setups to convert local currencies into dollars, customers burdened by high inflation or unstable monetary policies.
  • Complicated card agreements made country-by-country, inability to launch additional countries due to red tape.
Quantify this business impact:
  • How much money are you losing moving money around the globe?
  • How much time is your team spending to manually reconcile transfers?
  • How would your customers benefit if they had access to a dollar-denominated account?
  • If you had cards in the additional countries, how much more revenue would that be for you?
