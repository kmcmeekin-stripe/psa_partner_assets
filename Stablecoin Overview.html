What Are Stablecoins? A Simple Guide

Before diving in, let's break down the core concepts in simple terms.

Think of a Stablecoin as a Digital Dollar

Imagine a U.S. dollar that lives purely on the internet. That's a stablecoin. For every one digital dollar (like USDC or PYUSD) that exists, there is one real U.S. dollar held in reserve in a regulated bank. This 1-to-1 backing keeps its value stable, unlike other cryptocurrencies. It's not for speculation; it's for spending and sending, just like a regular dollar.

The Blockchain is Just a Shared Notebook

The technology that makes stablecoins work is called a blockchain. Think of it as a magical, public notebook. When you send someone a digital dollar, a new line is written in the notebook: "Alice sent Bob $10." Everyone can see this entry, it's permanent, and it can't be erased or altered. This creates trust and transparency without needing a bank in the middle to verify everything.
